# Paywalls

Paywalls are the primary interface for presenting subscription offers to your users. Helium provides flexible paywall creation and customization options.

## What are paywalls?

Paywalls are screens that display your subscription products and encourage users to subscribe. They can include:

- Product offerings and pricing
- Feature comparisons
- Promotional messaging
- Social proof and testimonials
- Call-to-action buttons

## Creating paywalls

Create paywalls in the Helium dashboard:

1. Navigate to **Paywalls**
2. Click **Create Paywall**
3. Choose a template or start from scratch
4. Customize design and content
5. Assign products or product buckets

## Paywall components

### Header

The top section typically includes:
- App icon or logo
- Headline
- Subheadline or value proposition

### Product display

Show subscription options:
- Product names and descriptions
- Pricing and billing periods
- Promotional badges (e.g., "Most Popular")
- Free trial information

### Features list

Highlight what users get:
- Feature bullets
- Icons or checkmarks
- Comparison tables

### Footer

Bottom section with:
- Purchase button
- Terms of service links
- Privacy policy links
- Restore purchases option

## Customization

### Visual design

Customize appearance:
- Colors and fonts
- Spacing and layout
- Images and icons
- Button styles

### Content

Tailor messaging:
- Localized text
- Dynamic product information
- Personalized offers
- A/B test variations

## Product assignment

Assign products to paywalls:

- **Direct assignment**: Specify exact products
- **Product buckets**: Use dynamic product groups that can be updated without app releases

See [Product buckets](/primitives/product-buckets) for more information.

## Localization

Support multiple languages and regions. See [Localization](/primitives/paywalls/localization) for details.

## Best practices

### Keep it simple

- Focus on 2-3 product options
- Use clear, concise copy
- Avoid overwhelming users with information

### Highlight value

- Lead with benefits, not features
- Use social proof
- Show what users get immediately

### Test and iterate

- Run experiments to optimize conversion
- Test different layouts and messaging
- Monitor analytics to identify improvements
